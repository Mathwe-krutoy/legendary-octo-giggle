Biography

Lil Peep (born Gustav Elijah Åhr on November 1, 1996) was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter who became a leading figure in the emo trap and post-emo revival music scene before his death from an accidental fentanyl and alprazolam overdose on November 15, 2017, at age 21. Known for his fusion of hip hop and emo, which explored themes of heartbreak and mental health, Peep built a dedicated fanbase and gained significant posthumous recognition for his unique style and contributions to the genre.
